"I will do it, though I do not know how," you say.

"My dear Arthur! Students really are amazing creatures. You can learn all that there is to know about their ways in a day and yet after a hundred years they can still surprise you." Slumblebore regards you with twinkling eyes and chuckles.

"As to the how: first you must deactivate the locking spell on the dungeon door with the magic password: watermelon."

"Watermelon?" you repeat.

"Yes: watermelon. I chose this password because it is my favourite fruit. The juicy red flesh, so tasty!... the visual contrast with the green outer skin.... the crunchy black pips - delicious!"

Slumblebore stares into the distance for a few moments then snaps out of his reverie.

"Where was I? Yes: watermelon. Then you must descend the staircase that leads down into the very depths of the school. In the past students would be imprisoned in the dungeons for misbehaviour. Now they are mainly used to store broken chairs and Christmas decorations. But we go down there so infrequently there is no telling what evil is brewing in some dark corner."

"I advise you to keep your wand at the ready and your wits sharp Arthur. And remember all the lessons, all the spells, the teachers have taught you. You will be put to the test and I do not know how it will turn out."

"Sir," you ask. "What do you think is down there?"

"Long have I studied this question, seeking knowledge in ancient scrolls and consulting with the wisest of the wise. Not a sausage. All I can say is that in the dead of night when I listen hard at the dungeon door I can sometimes hear a faint murmuring noise... like the ghosts of a thousand lost souls groaning at their fate. Either that or it's the hot water system."
