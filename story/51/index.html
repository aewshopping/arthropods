As you walk down the street you overhear all sorts of curious and wonderful snatches of conversation:

Watertomes is a small shop with an old wooden door and cramped dusty windows. You push the door open with a creak and enter hesitantly.

The inside is enormous: by some magic it is many times bigger than the outside. Brightly lit bookshelves stretch away from you to the left and right.

You see a boy with straight brown hair, about your age sitting on the floor, reading a book. He looks up at you as you walk in.
